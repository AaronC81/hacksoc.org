Weekly CS Seminars

Location and Times

CSE/102 at 13:15 every Wednesday.

Structure

We intend to pick textbooks and other such resources, and work through them over a period of time. Each seminar shall begin with a short presentation of the current topic, followed by discussion of that topic. Once it has been exhausted, we shall move on to general computer science and programming discussion.

It is not essential to have done the reading to attend the seminars, nor do we want it to be. If you want to do the reading beforehand and think about things before we all discuss it, that's great!

Content

Science Fiction Reading Group

As it is exam term, we didn't want to do anything too academic, or which required consistent attendance. Thus, we have a reading group, where we shall discuss computer science issues (if we want) raised in the stories.

There shall be no seminars in weeks 5, 6, and 7

We shall cover the following stories and topics in order:

  1. Reason (provability)
  2. The Reversed Man (safety-critical systems)
  3. The Last Question (machine learning, natural language processing, data mining, computability. provability)
  4. Guardian Angel (effects of an advanced civilisation contacting Earth)
  5. Break in weeks 5, 6, and 7
  6. The Nine Billion Names of God (brute-forcing, combinatorics)
  7. Robot Visions (replacing humans with machines)
  8. The Bicentennial Man (AI, special purpose vs. general purpose machines)
