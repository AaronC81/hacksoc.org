hacker [n.]

[originally, someone who makes furniture with an axe]

A person who enjoys exploring the details of programmable systems and how to stretch their capabilities, as opposed to most users, who prefer to learn only the minimum necessary. RFC1392, the Internet Users' Glossary, usefully amplifies this as: A person who delights in having an intimate understanding of the internal workings of a system, computers and computer networks in particular.

— The Jargon File

HackSoc is the University of York's Computer Science—or, more generally, computer enthusiast—society. We run mostly programming events: short series of lectures, seminars, competitions, projects, and the like. However, we're not just about the academic side of things, we also run socials! Boardgames and Cake and HackPub are our staple social activities, but we're also considering more possibilities, such as movie nights.

If any of this sounds interesting to you, great! We don't charge a membership fee, so feel free to just turn up to any events that catch your eye. If you'd like regular updates on what is happening in HackSoc, don't forget to sign up to our mailing list or follow us on Facebook and Twitter!

Committee

The HackSoc committee consists of a chair, secretary, and treasurer who must all be students, furthermore, there can be an arbitrary number of ordinary members who possess no voting powers in committee decisions, but can run events.
